Work Experience

I've had the opportunity to work as a programmer/developer at Sheridan College on a project funded by the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council (NSERC) and Encore Marketing Management. Under the leadership of Dr. Edward Sykes, our team's goal is to help sales representatives answer questions for products on store shelves to increase their productivity. The use of mobile devices will help them do so:

  • Sales rep goes to a store and take a picture of a store shelf with an iPad camera
  • Computer vision algorithm builds an object detector to recognize an object of interest
  • A bounding box surround an object of interest producing a touch point on the iPad
  • Sales rep touches the touch point and answers questions
To develop an object detector to detect the object, we are using the MATLAB computer vision toolbox to recognize products of interest on a store shelf. We've been using the Viola-Jones algorithm to develop object detectors for many products of interest. We've been able to detect juice boxes on store shelves and so far it has been a success.

The next stage is to build the iPad app that connects to a cloud-based service so a sales rep can use it in the field. We presented this project to the Sheridan Mobile Computing Symposium and won the grand prize for student creativity and innovation and won a cool $2500 between the three of us.
